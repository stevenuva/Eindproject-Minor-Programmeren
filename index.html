Agriculture and Population growth

How sustainable is the current population growth?

In 1798 demographer Thomas Malthus predicted that in the future there will come a day when there will not be enough food produced to feed everybody, and that too much land is being used for agriculture. This website will illustrate whether there were any indications between 1964 and 2014 that indicate that Malthus might be right and that overpopulation is or will be a real problem.

The power of population is so superior to the power of the earth to produce subsistence for man, that premature death must in some shape or other visit the human race.

Thomas Malthus in “An Essay on the Principle of Population (1798)”

Behind the Numbers

Overpopulation

Thomas Malthus predicted that the exponential population growth would create a situation whereby the human population would outgrow its available resources (e.g. agricultural land). While Thomas Malthus theory on overpopulation is already more than two centuries old, there were still many people in the 1960’s and 1970’s that truly believed that overpopulation would become a real problem. In 1968, for example, a biologist named Paul R. Ehrlich from the Stanford University wrote a book called “The Population Bomb”. Dr. Ehrlich’s opening statement left no room for interpretation: “The battle to feed all of humanity is over.” In “The Population Bomb” it was also forecasted that millions of people around the world would starve to death in the 70s, and that densely populated countries as India were in big trouble. Ehrlich even believed that in the year 2000 England might have stop existing altogether and that “the end” was coming. By “the end,” he meant “an complete breakdown of the capacity of the planet to provide humanity its much needed resources. 3

Indications

Signs which could indicate whether overpopulation might indeed become a problem might be seen by looking at the current population growth trend, the total amount of food available, the kind of food being eaten, the amount of land available for agricultural land and the rate of deforestation. Other indicators also play a role, but these aren’t included on this website. Obviously it would be problematic when the population growth outgrows the growth in total food production. The kind of food eaten can also have an impact whether overpopulation growth might become a problem, for example eating meat has a bigger impact on our planet that eating vegetables and fruits. Finally because the amount of land available is not unlimited, the growth of agriculture contributes to the deforestation that has been taking place around the world. 4

Critics

While some historians have stated that Malthus’ prediction might have been an accurate prediction of the future of mankind prior to the Industrial Revolution, most historians now agree with many contemporary economist that technological advances in the agricultural sector and further improvements in hygiene and overall health have ensured that Malthus prediction has yet to be fulfilled. Since 1798, the year in which Malthus made his predictions, the human population has increased sevenfold (from approximately 1 to 7 billion). Malthus simply did not foresaw the massive increase in agricultural yields during the twentieth century. 5
